About The "The Time Machine"

"The Time Machine" is a science fiction novella written by H.G. Wells and published in 1895. The story follows the adventures of an unnamed Time Traveller who invents a machine that can travel through time.

The Time Traveller first travels to the year 802,701 AD, where he encounters two distinct races: the childlike and peaceful Eloi, and the brutish and cannibalistic Morlocks. He soon learns that the Eloi are actually the descendants of the ruling class, while the Morlocks are the descendants of the working class who were forced to live underground. The Time Traveller manages to escape the Morlocks and travel further into the future, where he witnesses the death of the sun and the end of all life on Earth.

"The Time Machine" is considered a classic work of science fiction and has been adapted into numerous films, television shows, and other media. It explores themes such as social class, evolution, and the fate of humanity.
