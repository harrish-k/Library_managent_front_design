Sudha Murty

Sudha Murty is a well-known Indian author, philanthropist, and social worker. She is a prolific writer who has written several books in English and Kannada, and her works have been translated into many languages. She is best known for her ability to weave stories that are both engaging and thought-provoking. In this essay, we will explore some of her major works, achievements, and books. Sudha Murty was born in 1950 in Shiggaon, Karnataka, India. She earned her Bachelor of Engineering degree in Electrical and Electronics from the B.V.B. College of Engineering and Technology in Hubli, Karnataka, and went on to complete her Masters in Computer Science from the Indian Institute of Science in Bangalore

In the early years of her career, Sudha Murty worked as a computer scientist and engineer. However, she later turned her attention towards social work and philanthropy. Along with her husband, Narayana Murthy, she founded the Infosys Foundation in 1996, which works towards supporting education, healthcare, and rural development in India. Sudha Murty is also a celebrated author, having written over 40 books. Many of her books are based on her personal experiences and observations. Her writing style is simple and straightforward, which makes it accessible to a wide audience. Her books cover a wide range of topics, including social issues, mythology, children's literature, and more.

Some of her notable works include "Wise and Otherwise," a collection of short stories that showcases the various aspects of human nature, "The Day I Stopped Drinking Milk," a book that explores the importance of humility and empathy, and "How I Taught My Grandmother to Read," a heartwarming story about the bond between a grandmother and her granddaughter Sudha Murty has received several accolades for her work, including the Padma Shri, the fourth-highest civilian award in India, and the R.K. Narayan Award for Literature. She has also been recognized for her contributions to social work and philanthropy, and has been awarded the prestigious Philanthropist of the Year award by the NDTV Group. In conclusion, Sudha Murty is a remarkable individual who has made significant contributions to literature, social work, and philanthropy. Her books are a testament to her ability to tell stories that resonate with readers of all ages and backgrounds. Her achievements and accolades speak to her impact on Indian society, and her work continues to inspire and influence others.
